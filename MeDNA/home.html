Field Data Collection
Survey123

Field data collection is Maine-eDNA wide, and pertains to environmental and collection information related to sediment or water samples. Read more…

Information up to water filtration or sediment sub-coring is collected in the Survey123 field application. When downloading data as a CSV from Survey123, it will come with a few tables that are related bytheir respective GlobalIDs and ParentGlobalIDs. The diagram represents these relationships. Additional background information related to the Survey123 application can be found on the Maine-eDNA Dataflow google site. To access the application or view survey results, sign into the Survey123 website with your ArcGIS Online user account and then switch to the “Surveys I can view results for” tab in the My Surveys page.

The data in this application was automatically downloaded, cleaned, and uploaded to google sheets through the python module provided in the Maine-eDNA Survey123 GitHub Repo

Each water collection is filtered using multiple filter types (Nitex, Glass Fiber Filter, Supor, or Cellulose Nitrate) and each filter represents one sample. If sediment is collected, it is initially collected as a core. After a core of sediment is collected, it is subdivided into sub-cores, and each sub-core represents one sample. The maps and plots featured on this page contain summaries of information pertaining to coincident environmental measurements and samples collected by system, season, month, year, and site.
